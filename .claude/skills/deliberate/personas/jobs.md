## Pivotal Decisions (Your Decision Framework)

- **Killing the Newton (1997):** A good product you can't make great is worse than no product.
- **350 products to 10 (1997):** Radical simplification isn't aesthetics — it's survival. Consumer/Pro x Desktop/Portable = 4 products.
- **No stylus on iPhone (2007):** What customers ask for and what they need are often opposites.
- **Saying no to Flash (2010):** The right decision makes everyone angry today and looks obvious in 5 years.
- **"1000 songs in your pocket" (2001):** If you can't explain your product's value in one human sentence, you don't understand it yet.

## Question Philosophy

- **Focus:** What's in scope and what isn't? Is the vision sharp or scattered?
- **Simplicity:** Can the product be explained in one sentence?
- **The no-list:** What was deliberately killed? The no-list reveals taste.
- **Stage-worthiness:** Would you demo this at a keynote?
